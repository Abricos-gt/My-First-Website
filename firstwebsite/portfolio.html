★"Vaccination Coverage Survey" with Médecins Sans Frontières-OCA
(Operational Center Amsterdam)

During my time as a refugee in Sudan, I had the opportunity to work with Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF)
on vaccination coverage surveys in a refugee camp. This experience not only deepened my understanding
of healthcare in humanitarian settings but also allowed me to develop key skills in teamwork, resilience,
and communication. Despite the challenging environment, I was able to contribute effectively to the success
of the vaccination campaign.


•Leadership and Initiative:

As part of the MSF vaccination coverage survey team, I took on leadership responsibilities within the field.
I not only contributed to data collection and interviews but also took the initiative in organizing and
coordinating smaller teams for specific tasks. I led a team of data collectors, ensuring they understood
the survey objectives, proper procedures, and the importance of maintaining ethical standards when interacting
with vulnerable populations. My ability to motivate and guide others, especially in a high-pressure environment,
helped ensure smooth operations and the successful completion of our mission.


•Teamwork and Collaboration:

As part of the MSF vaccination coverage survey team, I collaborated closely with a diverse group of healthcare professionals,
fieldworkers, and local staff. Our team was composed of individuals from various backgrounds, and I quickly learned the
importance of effective communication and mutual respect. My ability to work cohesively with others was essential in ensuring
smooth data collection, reaching remote areas of the camp, and addressing logistical challenges. This experience reinforced my belief
in the power of teamwork, especially when resources are limited.


•Working in a Harsh Environment:

The refugee camp in Sudan posed numerous challenges, including extreme weather conditions, limited resources, and security concerns. Despite these
difficulties, I was able to remain focused and dedicated to the mission. I was responsible for conducting surveys, interviewing families, and
gathering vaccination data from individuals who often faced barriers such as fear, misinformation, or distrust of medical services. My resilience
and ability to adapt to the difficult environment ensured that we could gather meaningful data that would help improve healthcare delivery in the camp.


•Adaptability and Problem-Solving:

Working in such an unpredictable setting required constant adaptability. Whether it was navigating logistical challenges, managing cultural sensitivities, or
adjusting to rapidly changing conditions, I learned to quickly adapt my approach to meet the needs of the community. For example, when faced with low
vaccination card availability, I found alternative ways to gather accurate information by building trust with families and caregivers. This adaptability
and problem-solving mindset were crucial to ensuring that our surveys provided accurate and reliable data.

•Resilience in Overcoming Challenges:

The work in the refugee camp required not just physical endurance but emotional resilience. As a fellow refugee, I understood the difficulties faced by the community,
which allowed me to empathize with families and individuals we were helping. My personal experience as a refugee helped me connect with others and engage in
meaningful conversations, ensuring that people felt comfortable sharing vital health information. Despite the emotional and physical challenges, my ability to stay
focused and committed to the mission was key to the success of the survey.


•Strong Communication Skills:

Effective communication was essential throughout the survey process. I was often the first point of contact for individuals and families in the camp, explaining the purpose
of the vaccination survey and addressing any concerns. I made sure to communicate in a clear, respectful, and culturally sensitive manner, ensuring that people understood
the importance of vaccination and the survey itself. My communication skills not only helped in gathering accurate data but also played a crucial role in fostering trust
between the refugee community and the healthcare workers.


•Impact and Results:

The results of the survey provided valuable insights into vaccination coverage in the camp. Through the data we collected, MSF was able to identify areas with low immunization rates,
allowing for targeted vaccination campaigns and improved healthcare delivery. My leadership in coordinating the team, ensuring smooth communication, and managing challenges helped
achieve these results, contributing to the betterment of healthcare services for the refugee population.

★UNICEF -Health Promotion and Nutrition Support

In my role at UNICEF, I was involved in the MUAC (Mid-Upper Arm Circumference) screening process, primarily focusing on assessing acute malnutrition in children and pregnant women.
here's a breakdown of my responsibilities:


•MUAC Screening:

Objective: The MUAC screening tool is used to assess the nutritional status of individuals, particularly children under 5 years and pregnant women.
A simple tape measure is used to measure the circumference of the upper arm, and based on the measurement, we identify if the individual is malnourished
or at risk of malnutrition.
Process: I would conduct MUAC screenings in various community settings, such as health centers or outreach clinics, recording measurements to identify
individuals with acute malnutrition (classified into different categories based on severity).


•Supporting Community Health Programs

Health Education: After screening, I would educate caregivers (parents or family members) about the risks of malnutrition, the importance of proper nutrition,
and ways to prevent it, including breastfeeding practices, food fortification, and balanced diets.
Supplementary Feeding: For those identified as malnourished, I would ensure that they receive appropriate nutritional supplements
(e.g., Ready-to-Use Therapeutic Food or Plumpy’Nut) to help address their nutritional deficiencies and stabilize their condition.
Referral Services: If any cases were identified as severe or requiring advanced medical intervention, I referred them to the appropriate
healthcare facility for further management.


•Monitoring and Reporting

I played a role in tracking the progress of the individuals receiving food supplements and ensuring that they were benefiting from the intervention.
Data Collection: Recording MUAC measurements, tracking malnutrition rates, and other related health metrics were part of my daily duties.
This data would then be compiled and reported to higher management to ensure timely interventions.

Adaptability:

The conditions in the refugee camp were often unpredictable and extremely difficult. With limited resources, infrastructure, and access to essential services like education
and healthcare, I had to constantly adapt to the environment. Living in such a setting required developing resilience and the ability to make the most of what little was

Teamwork:

In the camp, teamwork was not a choice, but a necessity. The close-knit nature of the refugee community meant that we had to support each other constantly. With no formal education system in place,

us to learn from one another. We worked together to create small communal spaces, share food, and take care of each other’s well-being. My role often involved coordinating these informal initiatives
and encouraging others to contribute to collective efforts. The power of teamwork in such a setting became clear—without it, survival and mental well-being would have been far more difficult.


Communication:

Effective communication was essential for survival and creating a sense of community. Without access to formal education, we had to rely on creative and alternative methods to communicate, share information,
and organize. Whether it was discussing food distribution, healthcare, or safety issues, I learned how to communicate clearly and empathetically in such a resource-limited setting. I also found ways to
communicate across language barriers, as the camp hosted people from many different regions and backgrounds. I often acted as a liaison, helping to disseminate information, translating for those who didn't
speak the dominant languages, and ensuring that everyone understood their rights and available services.Living in the refugee camp and lacking access to basic resources, including education, reinforced my
understanding of the critical role adaptability, teamwork, and communication play in overcoming adversity. These skills not only helped me survive but also allowed me to contribute to creating a more supportive
environment for those around me.
